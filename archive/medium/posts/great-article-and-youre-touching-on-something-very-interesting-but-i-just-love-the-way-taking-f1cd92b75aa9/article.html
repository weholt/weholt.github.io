Great article and you’re touching on something very interesting, but I just love the way taking pictures using my Fuji cameras makes me feel. My phone doesn’t do that for me, no matter how many fancy filters or fake AI assisted bokeh it throws into my photos. For me picking up a real camera, with a proper lens, with gorgeous glass with character, is a totally different feeling than taking snapshots with my phone. It’s nice and handy to always have my phone on me and that’s why lots of my photos are taken using the phone, but my photos, my real photos, are taken with a real camera and the process leaves me much more satisfied than using a smartphone. Still, it’s just a matter of time until you can buy a filter that gives you the characteristics of a famous lens with legendary bokeh. Some people say it’s only the final product that’s important, not how you got there, but for me, as a wannabe photographer who was there when that moment happend and took specific decisions to find the proper exposure, aperture etc, — the process is just as important and that process isn’t possible with a phone.
